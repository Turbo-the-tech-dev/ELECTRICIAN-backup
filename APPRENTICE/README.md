# APPRENTICE Resources

Welcome to the APPRENTICE section of the ELECTRICIAN project! This directory contains resources specifically designed for electrical apprentices who are beginning their journey in the electrical trade.

## Overview

The apprentice program is the foundation of a successful electrical career. This section provides essential learning materials, safety guidelines, and practical tools to help apprentices develop the skills and knowledge required to become licensed electricians.

## Subdirectories

### LEARNING_MATERIALS
Resources for foundational electrical knowledge including:
- Basic electrical theory
- Ohm's Law and power calculations
- Circuit analysis
- Electrical formulas
- Print reading basics
- Introduction to codes and standards

### PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)
Guidelines and information about safety equipment required for apprentice electricians:
- Hard hats and safety glasses
- Insulated gloves and tools
- Arc-rated clothing
- Fall protection equipment
- Lockout/tagout procedures

### TOOLS
Essential tools every apprentice should know about:
- Hand tools (pliers, screwdrivers, wire strippers)
- Power tools (drills, saws, conduit benders)
- Testing equipment (multimeters, non-contact testers)
- Specialty tools (fish tape, cable pulls)
- Tool maintenance and care

### OTHER
Additional resources for apprentices:
- Study guides for licensing exams
- Job site etiquette
- Professional development
- Mentorship resources

## Getting Started

New apprentices should begin with the LEARNING_MATERIALS directory to build a strong foundation in electrical theory before moving on to practical applications.

## Safety First

Always follow proper safety procedures and consult with your supervisor when working with electrical systems. Remember: there's no substitute for proper training and supervision.
